Verse

यच्चापि सर्वभूतानां बीजं तदहमर्जुन |
न तदस्ति विना यत्स्यान्मया भूतं चराचरम् || 39||

Verse

yach chāpi sarva-bhūtānāṁ bījaṁ tad aham arjuna
na tad asti vinā yat syān mayā bhūtaṁ charācharam

word-to-word

yat—which; cha—and; api—also; sarva-bhūtānām—of all living beings; bījam—generating seed; tat—that; aham—I; arjuna—Arjun;
na—not; tat—that; asti—is; vinā—without; yat—which; syāt—may exist; mayā—me; bhūtam—creature; chara-acharam—moving and nonmoving

Translation

I am the generating seed of all living beings, O Arjun. No creature moving or non-moving can exist without Me.

Summary

In verse 39 of Chapter 10 in the Bhagavad Gita, Lord Krishna speaks about His divine manifestations. He declares that He is the seed of all existences and that nothing, whether moving or non-moving, can exist without Him. Essentially, this verse emphasizes that everything in the universe is rooted in and dependent upon the divine presence of Krishna. This concept ties into the larger theme of Chapter 10, which highlights Krishna's omnipresence and the divine qualities that pervade the entire cosmos. It's a beautiful reminder of the interconnectedness of all life through a divine essence.
