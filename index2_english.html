Chapter 11 of the Bhagavad Gita, titled "Vishwarupa Darshana Yoga" or "The Yoga of the Vision of the Universal Form," describes the revelation of Lord Krishna's cosmic form to Arjuna. At Arjuna's request, Krishna displays his Vishwarupa, a magnificent, all-encompassing form that embodies the entire universe—past, present, and future.
This form showcases countless divine manifestations, infinite faces, arms, and forms, radiating boundless energy and glory. Arjuna is both awestruck and overwhelmed by the sheer grandeur and terrifying aspects of this form, as he sees creation, preservation, and destruction occurring simultaneously within Krishna. The vision reinforces Krishna's supreme divinity and his role as the ultimate controller of the universe.
Arjuna surrenders to Krishna with profound devotion, and Krishna advises him to abandon all doubts, perform his duties selflessly, and dedicate his actions to the divine. This chapter emphasizes surrender, devotion, and recognizing God’s omnipresence.
