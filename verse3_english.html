Verse

एवमेतद्यथात्थ त्वमात्मानं परमेश्वर |
द्रष्टुमिच्छामि ते रूपमैश्वरं पुरुषोत्तम || 3||

Verse

evam etad yathāttha tvam ātmānaṁ parameśhvara
draṣhṭum ichchhāmi te rūpam aiśhwaraṁ puruṣhottama

word-to-word

evam—thus; etat—this; yathā—as; āttha—have spoken; tvam—you; ātmānam—yourself; parama-īśhvara—Supreme Lord; draṣhṭum—to see;
ichchhāmi—I desire; te—your; rūpam—form; aiśhwaram—divine; puruṣha-uttama—Shree Krishna, the Supreme Divine Personality

Translation

O Supreme Lord, You are precisely what You declare yourself to be. Now I desire to see Your divine cosmic form, O Greatest of persons.

Summary

In verse 3 of Chapter 11 in the Bhagavad Gita, Arjuna requests Lord Krishna to reveal His universal form. Arjuna acknowledges that Krishna is the supreme divine personality and that he is eager to witness Krishna's vast and magnificent form that encompasses all of creation. This verse marks a pivotal moment where Arjuna seeks a direct vision of Krishna's omnipresence and divine power.
