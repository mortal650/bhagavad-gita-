नान्तोऽस्ति मम दिव्यानां विभूतीनां परन्तप |
एष तूद्देशत: प्रोक्तो विभूतेर्विस्तरो मया || 40||

Verse

nānto sti mama divyānāṁ vibhūtīnāṁ parantapa
eṣha tūddeśhataḥ prokto vibhūter vistaro mayā

word-to-word

na—not; antaḥ—end; asti—is; mama—my; divyānām—divine; vibhūtīnām—manifestations; parantapa—Arjun, the conqueror of the enemies;
eṣhaḥ—this; tu—but; uddeśhataḥ—just one portion; proktaḥ—declared; vibhūteḥ—of (my) glories; vistaraḥ—the breath of the topic; mayā—by me

Translation

There is no end to My divine manifestations, O conqueror of enemies. What I have declared to you is a mere sample of My infinite glories.

Summary

In verse 40 of Chapter 10 in the Bhagavad Gita, Lord Krishna acknowledges that His divine manifestations are vast and limitless. He mentions that what He has described so far is only a fraction of His infinite divine forms. This verse underscores the boundless nature of Krishna's divine presence and powers, emphasizing that it is impossible to comprehensively enumerate all of His manifestations. This passage complements the preceding verses by reinforcing the idea of Krishna's omnipresence and the vastness of the divine.
