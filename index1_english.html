Chapter 10 of the Bhagavad Gita, titled "Vibhuti Yoga" or "The Yoga of Divine Glories," highlights Lord Krishnaâ€™s supreme nature and his manifestations throughout creation.
Krishna reveals to Arjuna that he is the ultimate source and sustainer of everything in existence, and all beings and objects arise from his divine energy. To help Arjuna understand his omnipresence, Krishna lists examples of his manifestations,
such as being the Sun among celestial bodies, Ganga among rivers, Vishnu among the Adityas, and the mind among senses. He explains that whatever is majestic, glorious, or powerful in the world reflects a fragment of his divine splendor.
This chapter inspires awe and devotion, encouraging Arjuna (and all devotees) to see the divine presence in every aspect of creation and to cultivate a deeper spiritual connection with God. Through this realization, Krishna guides his followers toward wisdom, devotion, and ultimate liberation.
